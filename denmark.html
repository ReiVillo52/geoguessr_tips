Language

Danish language uses the special letters: Æ, Ø, and Å.

Norway: Æ, Ø, Å
Swedish: Å
Icelandic: Æ only
Finnish: Å

Example sentence:

"En sommermorgen gik jeg i parken og så en egern spise blåbær. Luften duftede frisk, og solskinet varmede mit ansigt. Jeg tænkte, at den finske natur er smuk i alle årstider."

("On a summer morning, I walked in the park and saw a squirrel eating blueberries. The air smelled fresh, and the sunshine warmed my face. I thought that Finnish nature is beautiful in all seasons.")

Landscape & Infrastructure

Norway is a mountainous country with numerous fjords along its coastline.

Finland and Denmark are predominantly flat, while Sweden features varied terrain with flat, hilly, and mountainous areas.

Norwegian Road Markings:
Small roads: Long dashed white outer lines
Large roads: Solid white outer lines + orange-tinted yellow center lines

Danish Road Signs:

Unique pipe frame design (bent around the sign) – exclusive to Denmark

Pedestrian signs:
-5 stripes
-First + last stripe touch the triangle’s edge

Danish Direction Signs:

  • Design: Low-mounted with distinctive pipe frame
  • Colors: Red/white or blue/white with integrated arrow
  • Details: Often show road numbers

Exceptions:

  • Larger signs use standard layout
  • Highways: blue / green backgrounds

Danish Bollards:

  • Design: White with dark orange stripe
  • Reflector: Yellow (front)
  • Note: Unique to Denmark; green variants exist
