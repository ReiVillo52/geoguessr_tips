Language

Polish is a Slavic language. It includes:

  • Ł with a stroke through it (unique to Polish)
  • Ą and Ę with a small hook (also found in Lithuanian)
  • Ć, Ń, Ó, Ś, Ź with an acute accent
  • Ż with a small dot (unique to Polish)

Example sentence:

Spacerując po parku, zauważyłem piękne, kolorowe kwiaty. Słońce delikatnie ogrzewało moją twarz, a ptaki śpiewały wesołe melodie. Lekki wiatr kołysał gałęziami drzew, tworząc przyjemny, relaksujący klimat. To był idealny dzień na odpoczynek i kontemplację.

Landscape & Infrastructure

Polish architecture shares many characteristics with other Central European styles. Buildings often feature simple stone walls coated in light or pastel-colored stucco. Roofs are typically covered in shades of orange, red, black, or brown, with smooth, uniform tiles that sometimes appear almost synthetic. In rural areas, some homes have corrugated metal roofs, a feature also seen in Baltic architecture.
Stand-alone single-family houses are widespread across Poland and often have a minimalist, geometric design. In larger cities, multi-story apartment blocks dominate the landscape. Many of these structures were built during the communist era and originally had a stark, utilitarian appearance. However, numerous buildings have since been modernized, giving them a fresher, more inviting look.

You can also observe "roads between trees" and "Christian shrines" (Poland is one of the most Catholic countries in Europe).

Road Signs & Lanes

Polish bollards have a red strip that wraps all the way around the bollard. This strip has a red reflector on the front (sometimes hard to see), and a white reflector on the back. Bollards sometimes have numbers on them.

Same design can be found in Zakarpattia Oblast in Ukraine.

Polish direction signs: green background and these signs use a distinct font, which is a good clue for Poland.

Warning signs: triangular, yellow background and distinct thin red border
Prohibitory signs: round, white background, thicker red border
Pedestrian sign: unique, single horizontal line instead of the usual zebra stripes
Town entry signs: two, green one with the location name, the other one white with a stylized urban area in black color

Guardrails: square (normal B-type) with red circular reflectors

Roads: white road lines and double middle lines are very common

Electricity poles: holes (holes often do not extend all the way to the bottom of the pole), Sometimes: two poles are joined together, or put in an A-frame shape, also you can find round concrete poles
