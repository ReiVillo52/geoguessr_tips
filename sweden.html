Language

A North Germanic language closely related to Danish and Norwegian, featuring the distinct letters Å, Ä, Ö in its alphabet.

Example sentence:

"En varm sommardag gick jag genom skogen och hörde fåglarna sjunga. Det doftade friskt av tallar, och solen sken genom lövverket. Sverige har vacker natur alla årstider!"

"On a warm summer day, I walked through the forest and heard the birds singing. It smelled fresh of pine trees, and the sun shone through the foliage. Sweden has beautiful nature in all seasons!"

Landscape & Infrastructure

Smaller roads in Sweden:
- White dashed outer lines (shorter than Norway's)
- No colored lines (unlike Norway/Finnish yellow)

Swedish Highway Lines:
- Outer lines appear solid from afar but use short segmented patterns
- Similar to Denmark's, but Swedish segments are non-rectangular

Road Signs & Lanes

Swedish Pedestrian Signs:
- Standard 4 white stripes (vs. Finland's 5)
- Some feature a female silhouette (unique variant)

5 stripes: Finland & Denmark (consistent)
Norway: Usually 4, sometimes 5 (mixed)

Swedish Road Signs:
yellow background with red border

Swedish Directional Signs:
Blue background with white text and a thin directional arrow
