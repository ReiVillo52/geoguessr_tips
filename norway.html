Language

Norwegian uses the special letters: Æ, Ø, and Å.

Denmark: Æ, Ø, Å
Swedish: Å
Icelandic: Æ only
Finnish: Å

Example sentence:

"En sommermorgen gikk jeg i parken og så et ekorn som spiste blåbær. Luften luktet frisk, og solskinet varmet ansiktet mitt. Jeg syntes at den finske naturen er vakker i alle årstider."

("On a summer morning, I walked in the park and saw a squirrel eating blueberries. The air smelled fresh, and the sunshine warmed my face. I thought that Finnish nature is beautiful in all seasons.")

Landscape & Infrastructure

Norway is a mountainous country with numerous fjords along its coastline.

Finland and Denmark are predominantly flat, while Sweden features varied terrain with flat, hilly, and mountainous areas.

Norwegian Road Markings:
Small roads: Long dashed white outer lines
Large roads: Solid white outer lines + orange-tinted yellow center lines

Norwegian road markings often consist of small painted dots rather than continuous lines.

Road Signs & Lanes

Norwegian pedestrian signs:
4 stripes + simple silhouette

White "Sone" signs with speed limits mark urban zones in Norway.

Norwegian traffic signs feature a white background with red outline.

Most small Norwegian road signs use four rectangular bolts, though framed signs appear in cities.

Norwegian directional signs:
Yellow background with black borders, displaying road numbers in:
green boxes (European highways/major roads) or white boxes (smaller roads).
