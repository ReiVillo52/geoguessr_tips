Language

Latvian Language:
Baltic family, closest to Lithuanian
Unique letters:
Macron vowels: Ā, Ē, Ī
Hook consonants: Ģ, Ķ, Ļ, Ņ

Example sentence:

"Vienu vasaras rītu es pastaigājos parkā un ieraudzīju vāveri, kas ēda mellenes. Gaiss smaržoja svaigi, un saules stari sildīja manu seju. Es nodomāju, ka somu daba ir skaista visās gadalaikās."

("In the morning mist, I quietly walked down the road, watching the sun slowly rise over the fields. The air smelled like spring, and birds were singing joyfully.")

Landscape & Infrastructure

Traditional Latvian houses often combine wooden structures with white brick, featuring steep corrugated metal roofs. Modern variations include:

  • Pastel-colored stone walls
  • Mixed-material designs (wood + white brick)


Latvian Bollards:

  • Shape: Thin and slightly curved design
  • Reflectors:
    • Front: One rectangular reflector
    • Back: Two circular reflectors
    • Colors: White or Orange
  • Black Strip: Angled diagonally
  • Markings: Often includes numbers below the front reflector

Latvian Utility Poles:
Poletops: Use hook-shaped insulators arranged
in an alternating left–right–left pattern (or mirrored).

Baltic Kilometer Markers:
Same red design in all Baltic countries.

Latvia:
Placement: Parallel to the road.
Design: Number displayed on one side only.

Road Signs & Lanes

Latvian Pedestrian Signs:

  • 5 stripes (unique in the Baltics)
  • Long-legged silhouette — noticeably different from standard EU signs

Latvian Road Signs:

  • Thick red border + thin white outline (unlike Estonia’s no-outline design)
  • "ZONA" may appear below certain signs (e.g., speed limits), indicating a zone-based regulation

Latvian Directional Signs:
Blue background with white text
Arrow style: More stylized
(Compared to Estonia / Lithuania where
the arrowhead’s back is vertically cut)
